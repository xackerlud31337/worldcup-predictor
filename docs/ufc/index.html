      How this predictor works

      Every UFC bout ever fought (8,700+ of them) feeds a per-fighter Elo rating: beat someone strong and your rating jumps; lose to a nobody and it drops, with knockouts and submissions moving ratings more than decisions. The win probability comes from the Elo gap adjusted for ring rust and age: long layoffs and being the older fighter both count against you, with the size of those penalties fitted on thousands of past bouts and verified on fights the model had never seen. On top of that, each fighter carries a finish profile describing how their wins happen (KO, submission, decision, and in which round) and a victim profile describing how they tend to lose. Mixing the winner's finishing tendencies with the loser's vulnerabilities (smoothed toward league-wide base rates so small samples don't scream 100%) gives the method and round predictions. Career stats (strikes landed and absorbed per minute, takedowns, submission attempts) are shown for the eye test. Everything runs in your browser; honest caveat: MMA is the most upset-prone major sport, one punch changes everything, and even the best models hover around 60% accuracy. Treat these as informed estimates, not prophecy.

      What the numbers mean

      Elo rating
      A skill score updated after every bout based on the opponent's strength and the result. League average is 1500; champions sit around 1750–1850. With the ring-rust and age adjustments the model picked the winner in about 61% of recent bouts it had never seen, a strong result for a results-only model in a sport this chaotic.
      SLpM / SApM
      Significant strikes landed / absorbed per minute of fight time. High SLpM with low SApM is the striker's ideal; the reverse is a red flag.
      TD & Sub per 15 min
      Takedowns landed and submission attempts per 15 minutes, the grappling pressure a fighter brings per full fight's worth of time.
      Method & round
      How the fight ends, probability-wise: each fighter's own finishing habits blended with how the opponent tends to lose, smoothed toward league base rates. Round probabilities are shown for finishes only; "goes the distance" covers the rest.
      ⟳ Fetch latest
      Downloads the newest community UFC dataset (~10 MB, the same source the site is built from) and rebuilds every rating, record and profile in your browser. The dataset is community-maintained and usually updates within days of an event; fights more recent than its last update can't appear until it refreshes. The win-probability coefficients stay from the verified backtest.
      5-round fight
      Main events and title fights run 5 rounds instead of 3; more time means more finishes and different round math. Flip this on for main events.
      Betting view: fair odds
      Model probability converted to decimal odds (1 ÷ probability), the break-even price. A bookmaker's offer is only mathematically good if it's higher than the fair odds shown. Not betting advice.
