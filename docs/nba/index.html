      How this predictor works

      Every NBA game since 2002 (regular season and playoffs, 30,000+ of them) feeds a per-team Elo rating: beat a strong team and your rating jumps, lose to a weak one and it drops, with blowout wins moving ratings more than squeakers. Ratings carry between seasons but get pulled a quarter of the way back to average each summer, because rosters change. The Elo gap (plus a fitted home-court bonus) converts into an expected point margin with a known spread, and that one bell curve produces everything you see: the win probability, the fair point spread and the victory-margin odds, so the numbers can never contradict each other. The game total comes from each team's recent scoring and defensive rates, with the freshest games weighted heaviest. Everything runs in your browser; honest caveat: the model only sees results, not injuries, trades or rest days. Treat these as informed estimates, not prophecy.

      What the numbers mean

      Elo rating
      A skill score updated after every game based on the opponent's strength, the result and the margin. League average is about 1500; champions usually sit 1700+. On recent seasons the model had never seen, it picked the winner in roughly two thirds of games, about as good as results-only NBA models get.
      Predicted score
      The most likely final score: the total comes from both teams' recent scoring rates, the gap between the two numbers from the Elo difference. Real games scatter around it by a dozen points either way, which the odds account for.
      Fair spread
      The handicap that makes the game a coin flip by the model. If a bookmaker's line differs a lot from it, the model disagrees with the market. That's either an edge or something the model can't see, like an injury report.
      Victory margin
      The chance the game lands in each winning-margin band, read off the same bell curve as the win probability.
      Venue
      Home court in the NBA is worth roughly 2.5 points, fitted from two decades of games rather than assumed. Pick who hosts, or a neutral floor for hypotheticals.
      Betting view: fair odds
      Model probability converted to decimal odds (1 ÷ probability), the break-even price. A bookmaker's offer is only mathematically good if it's higher than the fair odds shown. Not betting advice.
